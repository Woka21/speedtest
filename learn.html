How the test works

Our browser-based speed test downloads 100 MB of random, incompressible data from a Cloudflare edge server. We time every chunk and report the 90th-percentile speed – the rate you can actually sustain, not a short burst.

Step-by-step process

  1. Your browser requests __down?bytes=100000000 (100 MB).
  2. Server streams data over HTTPS (HTTP/3 if available).
  3. Client measures bytes per millisecond.
  4. We discard the slowest 10 % (TCP slow-start) and average the rest.
  5. Result shown in Mbps and on the live gauge.

Factors that affect speed

  • Wi-Fi signal: distance, walls, interference (microwaves, baby monitors)
  • Device limits: old Wi-Fi 4 chips cap at ~50 Mbps
  • ISP peering: some providers throttle streaming ports in evening hours
  • Background apps: cloud backups, Windows updates, Dropbox sync
  • Ethernet vs Wi-Fi: cable eliminates radio interference

ISP plans & advertised speeds

ISPs advertise “up to” speeds. ACCC (Australia) and OFCOM (UK) require providers to publish peak-time averages – look for those numbers before you buy.

How to improve your speed

  1. Re-start modem/router – clears memory leaks.
  2. Update firmware – security + performance patches.
  3. Switch Wi-Fi channel – use 1, 6 or 11 on 2.4 GHz; auto on 5 GHz.
  4. Use 5 GHz or 6 GHz – less congested, higher throughput (shorter range).
  5. Limit background apps – pause cloud backups during calls.
  6. Upgrade plan – fibre > cable > DSL > satellite.

Troubleshooting flowchart

If your result is much lower than expected:

  1. Repeat test on cable → still low? → go to 2
  2. Reboot modem + router → still low? → go to 3
  3. Check firmware updates → still low? → go to 4
  4. Contact ISP with screenshots and timestamps
